A little bit about myself...

I've always believed that art and technology complimented one another, and found myself, even from a very young age, pursuing both as hobbies.
Throughout my college career I took advanced art courses alongside my computer science curriculum, and was often asked by classmates why I carried around an art portfolio when I planned to major in Computer Science. But there existed challenges in both mediums that I felt were worth the results of my hard work, and it felt satisfying to produce things that could either inspire others or assist them in their every day lives. I always felt that being able to put an artistic touch on a program helped with appealing to an audience, which I feel can be just as important as the program's function itself.
I've since graduated with my B.S. in Computer Science along with a B.A. in Spanish Language, and I am excited to see what else I can create for the world.

I really enjoy working on a team, but I do find independent projects comfortable as well. One of my biggest projects coming out of college, Digital Farmington Map, was done over the course of a semester with three other classmates, and I believe that our teamwork was essential to creating a product that our clients were proud of. I think it's incredibly important to be adaptable no matter the circumstances, and it helps me feel like a more effective team member if I am able to tackle group problems with a flexible mind.
